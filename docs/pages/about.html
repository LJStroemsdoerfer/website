About till.io

till.io is a project that started in the midst of the CoVid-19 pandemic. During the first months of the lockdown, we decided to something against the ever tedious ML deployment process.

Throughout the last years, the solution to ML deployment always seemed to be the emergence of an all-mighty deployment ecosystem. An ecosystem with it's own rules, own limitations, and of course also it's own benefits.

While full-fledged ecosystems often abstract difficult tasks, they also introduce challenges. First of all, they create a lock-in effect, as your code is always customized for your ecosystem. Second, they often force developers out of their comfort zone.

Maybe it's okay to leave your comfort zone, but the question remains, whether it is even necessary. Developers create their own ecosystems, within Python or R, or whatever they prefer. And these ecosystems are pretty powerful. Is it worth it creating an entirely new ecosystem, or should we just build on what developers already have.

At till.io we are clearly choosing the latter. Developers are amazingly produtive in the ecosystem that allows them to. Instead of enforcing a new ecosystem to handle tedious machine learning deployment tasks, we extend the comfort zone of Data Scientists. Without leaving R or Python, we allow Data Scientists to unlock the flexibility and power of Kubernetes, by simply extending their comfort zone.
